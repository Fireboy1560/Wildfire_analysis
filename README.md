1. Small fires, huge consequences - 0.2% of fires (class G) cause 74% of the total burned area.

2. Human activity dominates - over 85% of fires are caused by humans (burning waste, arson, equipment). Only 14.8% are caused by lightning.

3. Lightning – rare but dangerous – although only 14.8%, it causes the largest fires (average size 312 acres) and 6 of the 10 largest fires in history.

4. 2006 - record number of fires (114,004), and 2015 - record burned area (10.2 million acres) and average size (137 acres).

5. Where does it burn the most? - California leads in number of fires (189,550), but Alaska leads in average size (2,510 acres per fire). The western states burn more intensely.


6. June - the month of big fires—the average size then is 212 acres, twice the annual average.
